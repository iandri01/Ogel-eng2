🐾 The Beginning

The Dog Who Started It All

From One Puppy to More Than 190 Lives.

🐕
Before there was a shelter. Before there were rescue missions. Before there were 190 dogs and cats — there was one tiny puppy.
💛
Years ago, Amy Sukmana found a young female puppy alone on an empty piece of land. Her mother and littermates had died from Parvovirus. She was the only survivor. Amy brought her home.
✨
The veterinary treatment would cost nearly all of Amy's savings at the time. But giving up was never an option. The puppy survived. Amy named her Ogel. That one rescue changed everything.
🏡
What began with a single act of compassion eventually grew into Omah Ogel — a sanctuary that now cares for more than 190 rescued animals.

👩 The Founder

The Woman Behind Omah Ogel

Amy Sukmana is known throughout Indonesia's animal welfare community as someone who never hesitates to help.

🤝
For years, she has supported fellow rescuers, shared food and resources with struggling shelters, and offered guidance to those facing difficult rescue situations.
💼
While many shelters rely on public donations, Amy has spent years sustaining Omah Ogel through her own businesses — including a catering company, pet-related services, and animal transportation.
🌟
While helping others, Amy rarely asked for help herself. Today, those same businesses continue to help sustain Omah Ogel and the nearly 190 rescued animals who depend on it.
📋 The Story

From a Rented Shelter to a Forever Home

For six years, Omah Ogel operated from a rented property in Depok, West Java. It became a safe haven for rescued animals who had nowhere else to go.

!
April 4, 2026
The Notice Arrived
Following concerns raised by nearby residents, Omah Ogel was asked to relocate — with only six weeks to move nearly 190 rescued animals.
💛
The Decision
Leaving Them Behind Was Never an Option
Many of these animals are elderly, disabled, or recovering from illness. They had already experienced abandonment once before.
🏡
The Investment
A 1,051 m² Plot in Bogor Regency
Using years of personal savings, Amy purchased the land. For the first time, Omah Ogel has land of its own — a place where rescued animals won't have to move again.

The Land Is Ready. Now We Must Build.

Unlike many fundraising campaigns, the land has already been purchased using Amy's personal savings. On a 1,051 m² property in Bogor Regency, we are creating a safe, humane, and permanent sanctuary for rescued dogs and cats.
